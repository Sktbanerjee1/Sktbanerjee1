01 — About

At the intersection of computation and biology

Computational biologist with expertise in statistical genetics, multi-omics data integration, and algorithm development. Trained to handle complex biological and clinical datasets, including GWAS, single-cell transcriptomics, mass spectrometry, and clinical imaging, with a consistent focus on translating computational findings into testable biological hypotheses.

Committed to rigorous data governance, reproducibility, and the development of open, scalable analytical frameworks. Currently pursuing a PhD in Computational Biology at the University of Virginia, developing methods for causal gene identification through the integration of GWAS, eQTL, and single-cell transcriptomic data.

Previously contributed to clinical genomics pipelines at PierianDx, deep learning for medical imaging at CSIR-IGIB, and global digital health architecture for the Governing Health Futures 2030 initiative.

02 — Experience

Research & Professional Journey

Computational Biology Research Fellow Aug 2024 – Present
University of Virginia, Department of Genome Sciences
  • Developed a computational colocalization pipeline integrating GWAS (Diversity Outbred mice) and bulk RNA-seq eQTL data to identify candidate causal genes. Implemented conditional analysis with bootstrapping to validate concordance between conditional and colocalization signals at the most significantly associated loci.
  • Deconvoluted cell-type proportions from bulk RNA-seq data and constructed cell-type-specific WGCNA modules. Identified key hub genes using Bayesian network analysis (bnlearn), then prioritized candidate causal genes by overlapping colocalization hits with network hub genes.
  • Performed pseudotime trajectory analysis on single-cell RNA-seq data using Slingshot to characterize gene expression dynamics of prioritized causal genes along the differentiation trajectories of osteoblasts and osteoclasts in bone marrow-derived stromal cell populations.
  • Optimized parallelization pipelines on HPC clusters, reducing computational runtime by 24 hours per batch for genome-wide analyses.
Senior Data Analyst Sep 2022 – Jul 2024
University of Virginia, Department of Genome Sciences
  • Developed a high-throughput processing pipeline for Low-Pass Whole Genome Sequencing (LP-WGS), achieving a 15% reduction in genotyping costs while maintaining high data fidelity for downstream haplotype calling and eQTL analysis.
  • Deployed and managed a Proteogenomics pipeline (Long-Read Proteogenomics, Nextflow-based) integrating long-read RNA sequencing with mass spectrometry data, enabling identification of novel transcript isoforms and protein products absent from GENCODE reference annotations.
  • Architected a standardized data governance framework enforcing FAIR principles across all research data assets, transforming an unstructured environment into a reproducible, audit-ready ecosystem.
Bioinformatician, Assay Productization Oct 2021 – Jun 2022
PierianDx
